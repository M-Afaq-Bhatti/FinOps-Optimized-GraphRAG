India is a country in South Asia. It is the seventh-largest country by area and the most populous country in the world. New Delhi is the capital of India and serves as the seat of the central government.

The Indian economy is one of the fastest-growing major economies in the world. Mumbai is the financial capital of India and home to the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE), which is one of the oldest stock exchanges in Asia. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI), headquartered in Mumbai, controls monetary policy and regulates the banking sector.

Narendra Modi has served as Prime Minister of India since 2014. He leads the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) and has implemented several major economic initiatives including the Digital India program, Make in India manufacturing push, and the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) which has revolutionized digital transactions.

India became independent from British rule on August 15, 1947. The independence movement was led by Mahatma Gandhi, who championed nonviolent civil disobedience. Jawaharlal Nehru became the first Prime Minister of independent India.

The technology sector plays a crucial role in India's economy. Bengaluru, often called the Silicon Valley of India, is home to major IT companies and startups. Infosys, Wipro, and Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) are among the largest IT companies headquartered in India. These companies serve clients globally and contribute significantly to India's export earnings.

India has a diverse agricultural sector. The Green Revolution in the 1960s transformed Indian agriculture, making the country self-sufficient in food production. Punjab and Haryana are known as the breadbasket of India due to their high wheat and rice production.

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has achieved remarkable milestones. The Chandrayaan missions explored the Moon, while the Mangalyaan mission successfully reached Mars on its first attempt, making India the first Asian country to reach Mars orbit. ISRO's cost-effective approach has made India a competitive player in the global space industry.

Healthcare in India is provided through a mix of public and private institutions. The All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) is a premier public medical institution. India is a major producer of generic medicines and vaccines, earning the title of Pharmacy of the World.

India has a rich cultural heritage spanning thousands of years. The Taj Mahal in Agra, built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Bollywood, centered in Mumbai, produces the largest number of films annually of any film industry in the world.

The Ganges River is considered sacred by Hindus and flows through northern India. Varanasi, located on the banks of the Ganges, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and a major center of Hindu pilgrimage and culture.
